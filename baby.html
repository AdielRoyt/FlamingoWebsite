Lifecycle

  1. Hatching
    • Chicks hatch in 24-36 hours
    • Become strong enough to walk after around a week
  2. Juveniles
    • Become juveniles after about 9-13 weeks
    • Still have same gray feathers as babies
  3. Adults
    • Become adults at 1 to 2 years of age
    • Grow to be 30 to 50 inches tall

Most of the time, Flamingos only lay one large egg. The eggs range in size, between 78 by 49 mm, and
weigh about 115 to 140 grams. The incubation period for the egg is between 27 and 31 days.
Hatching takes between 24 and 36 hours. Baby chicks have either gray or white feathers, a
straight red bill, and red or pink legs. A flamingo chick is not very agile after hatching.
It can only push its wings or lift its head.Source
